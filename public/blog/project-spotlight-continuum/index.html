Project Spotlight: Continuum

One of the best parts of Hack the Valley is watching a weekend idea turn into something people actually use. Continuum — built by Hack the Valley winner Mofe — is exactly that: a project that started at the event and is now live in the Chrome Web Store and on Firefox Add-ons.

What it does

If you use AI chat tools, you have hit the wall: a conversation gets long, you run into message or token limits, and you lose all that built-up context. Continuum fixes that. It's a browser extension that captures an entire AI chat and resumes it in a fresh conversation — so the context carries over and you keep going.

It works across Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity, and you can even move a chat from one platform to another. The whole thing is local-first: no servers, no accounts, no tracking — your conversations stay on your device.

The features that stood out

  • One-click capture of a full conversation, including uploaded files and images.
  • Resume anywhere — pick up a chat in a new tab, or on a different AI platform entirely.
  • Export to PDF (with embedded images) or Markdown.
  • Optional AI compression that summarizes the long middle of a chat while preserving the decisions and files that matter.
  • An organized library of saved chats, grouped by platform.

Try it out

Continuum is free and open source. Grab it for your browser or dig into the code:

  • Chrome Web Store
  • Firefox Add-ons
  • GitHub repository

Continuum took home a win at Hack the Valley 2026 — and it's easy to see why. It's exactly what this event exists to grow: students in the Valley building real, useful tools and shipping them to the world. Huge congrats to Mofe — we can't wait to see what comes next.
